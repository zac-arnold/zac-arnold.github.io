Utilising your journal findings and the high level overview of the Core learnings you have covered in Foundations - answer each of the following questions:

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

I've learnt that when faced with a challenge I need to actively remind myself to be resilient. I've come to find that I work best when instructions and variables are defined from the beginning of a task and that regular meditation and reflection are the basis for a healthy work / life balance.

What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?

Values, empathy and self awareness play a huge role in learning and programming. I think that a lot of the internets problems are caused by not leading development with these traits.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

Being exposed to, and adopting Carol Dweck's 'Power of Yet' philosophy was a turning point for me. It's something that I remind myself constantly, especially if when I am struggling with a problem.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

I found the 'flipped learning' experience challenging because it's a totally new way of thinking. Ultimately I think it is the reason why I've been able to retain so much new information.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

I think EDA has a strong focus on core learning for multiple reasons. It preps students for days of problem solving based work, and helps them stay motivated through the accelerated learning environment.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practicing programming instead? Justify your answer.

Not at all. Knowing your personality, and how it works (especially within groups environments) can only benefit a student in the long run.

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

The long term goal is to find employment once I leave Dev Academy, so I can provide for myself, my partner and my family. I'd seek an opportunity to become a better coder by getting into situations where I can put in the hours needed to upskill. Ideally I'd like to work in sectors that deal in Music, Environmentalism, Education or the Union movement.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

I think that my evaluations of my strengths and limitations from Sprint 2 were accurate. I do work best when I adhere to a routine and I can minimise distraction. I've also discovered that I enjoy being in collaborative environment, and have struggled with the isolation of Foundations. I look forward to Bootcamp and re-arranging my routine to accommodate my new surroundings.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I think maintaining resilience in difficult learning environments and keeping focused will be my biggest non-technical challenges in Bootcamp.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

I'd like to develop my communication and self awareness skills. I think that will aid me best for when I pair with others or work in groups.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

I commit to being present throughout Bootcamp, taking time to asses the workload and to change my routine accordingly. I will move into all situations with an open mind and empathy.
